# ChinaReady Master Checklist

This document is the working backlog for ChinaReady: a fixed travel-kit product for foreign visitors arriving in China. The goal is to build a practical "first 24 hours in China" kit, then expand city maps, printed cards, hotel delivery, and optional add-on services one by one.

## Product Positioning

ChinaReady is not a souvenir box. It is an arrival survival kit for foreign travelers who need payment, internet, transport, maps, language help, restroom supplies, charging, and hotel check-in support before they fully understand China.

Core promise:

> Everything you need for your first 24 hours in China.

Early strategy:

- Sell fixed kits first, not free item selection.
- Build around real arrival friction, not generic travel items.
- Make maps, route cards, payment cards, internet cards, and bilingual help cards the main value.
- Use low-risk, lightweight physical items to support the information product.
- Avoid SIM cards, cash, medicine, food, liquids, and high-risk electronics in the first stage.
- Start with Shanghai, then expand to Beijing, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Chengdu, Xi'an, Hangzhou, and Chongqing.

## Research Signals To Keep In Mind

Official and traveler-facing sources point to the same friction areas:

- China is actively improving inbound tourism convenience across payment, communication, transport, sightseeing, shopping, ticketing, and accommodation.
- Alipay's international version supports overseas travelers binding international bank cards for mainland China use.
- WeChat Pay supports overseas visitors linking international cards, with small-payment fee-waiver policies announced through official Beijing service pages.
- Foreigners staying in hotels are registered by the hotel; foreigners staying outside hotels may need accommodation registration within 24 hours, with online pilot services in selected regions.
- Beijing's 2025 English Map adds service categories such as currency exchange, tax refund, SIM card services, transit card services, ID services, accommodation registration, and attractions.
- Traveler forums repeatedly mention payment setup, working maps, eSIM/VPN confusion, language barriers, tissue/restroom issues, hotel check-in, and train/attraction booking as real day-one problems.

## Do Not Sell In Stage 1

| Item | Why Not |
| --- | --- |
| Activated SIM cards | Real-name mismatch and resale risk. |
| Unclear-source SIM cards | Compliance and customer support risk. |
| Customer passport handling for SIM | Privacy, compliance, and liability burden. |
| Cash or coins | Loss, disputes, hotel delivery, and compliance uncertainty. |
| Medicine | Liability and regulatory risk. |
| Food | Shelf life, allergens, storage, and cross-border expectations. |
| Liquid toiletries | Leakage and shipping restrictions. |
| Power banks | Shipping restrictions, quality risk, and after-sales burden. |
| High-value electronics | Damage, return, warranty, and margin risk. |
| Fake branded goods | Legal and platform risk. |
